Phase 33 brings the two great Chinese text libraries. From the Kanseki
Repository: the classics, dynastic histories, masters, and literary
anthologies — some 3,500 texts fetched text-by-text under a polite,
resumable, catalog-driven design (the library's first many-repo
source), each citable to text, juan, and printed leaf. From CBETA: the
Taishō canon and the Xuzangjing in scholarly TEI, where a passage's
identity IS the classical citation — volume, page, register, line —
and the Chinese Āgamas ingested earlier now point into their canonical
home. The Thesaurus Linguae Sericae adds something the library has not
had before: an onomasiological concept net, three thousand concepts
with twenty thousand member words, as a dictionary shelf beside the
reconstruction lanes.

A crosswalk links the Kyoto Classical Chinese treebank to the very
Kanripo texts it annotates, so gold morphology and source editions
meet. Licensing as always: the canon-level gate holds CBETA at
non-commercial and refuses its four excluded collections by name; the
Kanripo organization grant is recorded verbatim with a confirmation
outstanding; every discrepancy found is journaled, none papered over.
