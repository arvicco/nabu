Phase 32 opens the library's Far Eastern axis, from Classical Chinese
to historic Japanese. The Kyoto treebank brings 86,239 gold-annotated
sentences of the Analects, Mencius, the Liji, the Chuci, and Tang
poetry behind one configuration row. The 272 Literary Chinese Āgama
texts of the Taishō canon — already on disk, held back only by a scope
rule — now mint beside their English translations, and SuttaCentral's
parallels graph arrives as reference edges, so the Kālāma Sutta and its
Madhyama-āgama counterpart meet in one `links` query across canons and
languages.

On the Japanese side, the Oxford-NINJAL Corpus of Old Japanese brings
all 4,991 texts of Old Japanese poetry — the complete Man'yōšū among
them — lemmatized and parsed, with the corpus's own lexicon as a
dictionary shelf beside it. The reconstruction shelf spans Baxter-Sagart
Old Chinese, the Guangyun critical edition of the Qieyun system (CC0),
and Wiktionary's dual reconstruction systems; the Unihan database,
KANJIDIC2/JMdict, and the Heian-period character dictionaries of the
HDIC project carry the Sinoxenic bridge — a character's readings from
Middle Chinese to go-on and kan-on.

As always: licenses read verbatim at ingestion (one genuine upstream
contradiction found and journaled for a ruling), censuses measured
against whole files, and every count dated. The Kanseki Repository and
CBETA are the planned continuations.
