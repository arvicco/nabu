The library nearly tripled today. As of **2026-07-19** the catalog holds
**737,299 documents / 11,357,320 passages** across **69 sources**, with
**711,376 dictionary entries** and a freshly censused lemma index of
**12.4 million gold rows in twenty-two languages** (plus 8.0 million
honestly labelled silver rows in eight).

Phase 31 is the Ancient Near East: the **TLHdig Hittite corpus** (23,486
manuscripts — more than 98% of the published Hittite fragments, CC BY),
the **CDLI universal catalog** (353,156 cuneiform artifacts, of which
135,201 transliterated — proto-cuneiform and proto-Elamite's only
machine-readable home), the **eBL Fragmentarium** (23,288 fragments with
inline English, cross-linked to CDLI), the **Copenhagen Ugaritic
Corpus**, the **Peshitta Old Testament** (taking `align "GEN 1.1"` to
seven witnesses), the **Digital Syriac Corpus** (632 documents), and the
**ETCSL** Sumerian literary canon beside ORACC's ePSD2 editions of the
same poems — two scholarly witnesses, deliberately unmerged. ORACC
itself grew to 38 projects with the Achaemenid royal trilinguals (Old
Persian and Elamite are new axis languages, with Ugaritic, Syriac, and
Eblaite) and the Ur III administrative mass.

Two corpora introduce the library's **silver lemma tier** at scale:
TLHdig's candidate morphology and CDLI's unlemmatized transliterations
never contaminate gold search — every hit carries its tier label, and
`--gold-only` excludes them.

As always, every count above was read from the live catalog on the date
given, and every text carries its upstream license on every surface.
