Phase 17 merged on 2026-07-13 (PR
[#21](https://github.com/arvicco/nabu/pull/21)) — the sources phase, four
adapters shipped in one day, written up retrospectively as this News
section opens.

**Coptic Scriptorium** brought Coptic in as the library's fifteenth
gold-lemma language (233,020 lemma rows at sync), with
language-of-origin loan tags that expose the Greek layer inside Coptic,
and two new witnesses for the parallel New Testament. **The Epigraphic
Database Heidelberg** added the Latin inscriptions of the Roman empire —
and with them the library's first genre facets (`search
--type/--province/--material`) and a 93,646-row persons prosopography.
**Monier-Williams** joined the reference shelf with bijective SLP1↔IAST
transcoding and citation tiers that resolve Rigveda references to their
source texts; its comparanda became the second etymological witness
beside the Wiktionary-derived shelves. **Wiktionary reconstruction, part
2** added four intermediate proto shelves (Balto-Slavic, Italic,
Indo-Iranian, West Germanic) and multi-hop closure through them, with
borrowings flagged honestly as "(loan)" in cognate lists. Three surveys
(Etruscan, Slovenian/Balkan Slavic, Indo-European lexical databases)
queued the next round of sources.
