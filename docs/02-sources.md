Verify licenses and endpoints at implementation time вАФ this table reflects the landscape as of mid-2026 and these projects do change terms and infrastructure.

## Ranking synthesis

**By effort-to-value (build order):** 1 вЖТ 2 вЖТ 4 вЖТ 3/5 вЖТ 10 вЖТ 7 вЖТ 8 вЖТ 11 вЖТ 6 вЖТ 9 вЖТ 15 вЖТ 14 вЖТ 18/19 вЖТ 13.

Rationale: adapters 1вАУ2 share one EpiDoc/CTS implementation and immediately deliver the classical backbone; 3вАУ5 share the PROIEL/CoNLL-U implementation and deliver the annotated IE layer; 10 reuses EpiDoc. That's three parser families covering ten sources. TITUS (#13) is deliberately late despite top-tier content: it's the highest-friction integration and should be attempted only once the normalization layer is mature enough to absorb its encoding chaos.

**By content criticality for the Slavic/IE research axis:** 3, 5, 19, 18, 13, 4 вАФ these are the corpora whose absence would actually block work.

**By licensing cleanliness (safe to ever share/publish derivatives):** 1, 2, 7, 8, 9, 10, 15, 16 (CC with attribution) > 3, 5 (NC clause) > 6, 14, 19 (research-use, keep private) > 13, 23вАУ27 (private use only, segregate).
